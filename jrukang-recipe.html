Moan Ang (Khmer Grilled Pork Belly)

Khmer-style grilled pork belly rubbed with galangal, lemongrass, and palm sugar — slow-grilled over wood fire until the skin is irresistibly crispy and the meat is juicy and deeply aromatic. A favourite at Cambodian celebrations.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

40 min.

serving

4 servings

Tags : Pork

instructions

step 1

Score the pork belly: Using a sharp knife, lightly score the skin of 800 grams pork belly (skin-on, sliced into thick strips) in a crosshatch pattern — this helps the marinade penetrate and the skin to crisp up beautifully.

step 2

Make the marinade: In a bowl, combine 2 lemongrass stalks (finely minced), 30 grams galangal (peeled and grated), 5 garlic cloves (minced), 3 shallots (finely chopped), 1 teaspoon fresh turmeric grated (or ½ tsp ground), 3 tablespoons palm sugar, 3 tablespoons fish sauce, 2 tablespoons oyster sauce, 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce, 1 teaspoon black pepper, and 2 kaffir lime leaves (finely shredded). Mix well into a thick, fragrant paste.

step 3

Marinate the pork belly: Rub the marinade all over the pork belly, pressing into the scored skin. Cover and refrigerate overnight, or at least 4 hours.

step 4

Make the dipping sauce: Whisk together 3 fresh red chili (sliced), 4 garlic cloves (minced), 3 tablespoons lime juice, 2 tablespoons fish sauce, and 1 teaspoon sugar in a small bowl. Taste and adjust — it should be bright, tangy, garlicky, and a little spicy. Set aside.

step 5

Grill low and slow: Heat your charcoal or wood grill to medium heat. Place pork belly strips skin-side down first. Grill slowly, turning occasionally, allowing the fat to render and the meat to cook through.

step 6

Glaze and crisp the skin: Brush 2 tablespoons honey over the pork belly and increase grill heat to high. Grill for a final few minutes, turning often, until the skin is crispy, caramelised, and deeply golden.

step 7

Rest and slice: Remove from the grill and rest on a board before slicing into bite-sized pieces.

step 8

Serve: Arrange sliced pork belly on a platter with fresh cucumber slices, steamed jasmine rice, and fresh herb sprigs (mint, basil, or coriander). Serve the dipping sauce alongside. Eat with fresh herbs and rice for the full Khmer experience!

ingredients

800 g pork belly, skin-on, sliced into thick strips

2 lemongrass stalks, finely minced

30 g galangal, peeled and grated

5 garlic cloves, minced

3 shallots, finely chopped

1 teaspoon fresh turmeric, grated (or ½ tsp ground)

3 tablespoons palm sugar (or brown sugar)

3 tablespoons fish sauce

2 tablespoons oyster sauce

1 tablespoon dark soy sauce

1 teaspoon black pepper

2 kaffir lime leaves, finely shredded

2 tablespoons honey (for glazing)

3 fresh red chili, sliced

4 garlic cloves, minced (for dipping sauce)

3 tablespoons lime juice

2 tablespoons fish sauce (for dipping sauce)

1 teaspoon sugar (for dipping sauce)

1 fresh cucumber, sliced (to serve)

2 cups steamed jasmine rice (to serve)

4 fresh herb sprigs — mint, basil, or coriander (to serve)

tools

Charcoal or Wood Grill (or Grill Pan)

Mixing Bowl

Sharp Knife & Cutting Board

Pastry Brush (for glazing)

Small Bowl (for dipping sauce)

Measuring Spoons & Cups
